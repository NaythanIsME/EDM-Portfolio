Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint

Ending Scene — The Oldest Dream

Chapter ??? — Beyond the Final Line

There was no stage. No message windows. No constellations. Only silence, and the space outside the story.

Kim Dokja floated alone, nameless and forgotten. He had become the reader no one remembered, the narrative no one could reach.

Kim Dokja “So this is where stories go when they end...”

He no longer had a physical form, only thoughts flickering like fading light. The world had moved on. The story was over.

[ Someone is writing your story. ]

Han Sooyoung (tls123) “Hey. I don’t care what the system says. This story isn’t finished until I say it is.”

Across dimensions, across time, someone was writing. The only person who had read his story to the end. The one reader left.

Han Sooyoung (tls123) “You told me once. That every reader changes the ending.”
Han Sooyoung (tls123) “Then watch me, Kim Dokja. I’m going to write you back into the world.”

The void trembled. The text that had once vanished began to return. A new beginning was forming — not from the system, but from a single reader's will.

Kim Dokja “...Sooyoung.”

[ You are no longer the Oldest Dream. ]

The world remembered him.

A tribute to the final chapter of “Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint” by Sing Shong.
Characters: Kim Dokja & Han Sooyoung
